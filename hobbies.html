Working Out

A few years back, Mark was given a physical and that day became a wake-up call. The physician told him his cholesterol was too high and that he needed to diet and exercise. Mark started simple with a jog every other day. This grew to adding weight training a couple of times a week. This meant he could still enjoy the food he wanted, but he had to work harder to get that luxury. He now runs 4 miles a day and weight training about three times a week. He hardly misses a day. He also participates in and coaches a company softball team that is undefeated.

Trying New Food

Growing up in the heart of New Orleans, Louisiana, Mark was introduced to Creole and Southern styles of cooking at a very young age. The medley flavors with a hint of heat never tired his taste buds. As he grew, he sought new foods to whet his appetite and satisfy his desire for something unique. Mark can often be found, even by himself, at a mom-and-pop or hole-in-the-wall type restaurant asking for the House Special. Buffets are a favorite as they give a lot of variety, but he will always go back to the comfort food of crawfish and gumbo.
